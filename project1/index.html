(Dis)Information

Interaction

At first, everything reads “information”—but as you “scratch,” some words underneath reveal something else: disinformation. These hidden messages were covered up, and only by interacting can you uncover what’s real and what’s misleading.

Description

This project looks like a wall of pure “information,” all seemingly the same. But hidden beneath some of these words is disinformation, camouflaged and quietly embedded. As you engage with the piece—scratching the surface—false signals begin to emerge. They don’t alert you with sounds or motion. Instead, your own action reveals the deception. The surface rubs away to show what was there all along.

Design Intention

This piece plays with the idea that not all disinformation is loud or obvious. Sometimes it hides in plain sight. By turning the screen into a scratchable surface, this work invites users to physically reveal what’s been covered up. It suggests that uncovering the truth isn’t always about flashy alarms—it’s about patience, attention, and willingness to dig deeper. With a simple gesture, users take the first step in separating noise from signal.
