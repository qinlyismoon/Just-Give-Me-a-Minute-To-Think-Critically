(Dis)Information

Interaction

If a word is flagged as disinformation, it flickers, turns red, and shakes slightly—an alarm is triggered. Clicking on it causes it to shatter into particles.

Description

This piece displays a field of repeating “information.” Some words, however, are actually disinformation in disguise. TITAN acts like a detector: when disinformation is spotted, it’s visually disturbed—highlighted in red and set in motion. With a simple click, these false signals break apart and disappear, leaving the true information behind.

Design Intention

This work imagines TITAN as a powerful guide—helping users to question it. When something feels off, it shows you. With your action, the noise can be cleared. Through interaction, users are invited to see how truth and falsehood can visually overlap—and how critical thinking, with help, can peel one from the other.
