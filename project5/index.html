Customized TITAN

Interaction

Three sliders—aperture radius, blurred edges, and gray level—allow users to simulate a flashlight effect, gradually unveiling the masked TITAN beneath the surface.

Description

Rather than presenting TITAN as a static entity, the work emphasizes TITAN’s adaptability: users can choose between three distinct coaching styles—Short Coach, Agentic Coach, and Extensive Coach—each offering a different tone and degree of guidance. This customization reflects the larger design proposition: users should retain epistemic agency in how they engage with intelligent systems.

Design Intention

This piece plays with the idea that “seeing clearly” takes effort. Instead of revealing TITAN all at once, users have to slowly uncover it by adjusting light-like filters—just like how we often need to look closer to understand what information really means.

The three slider controls act like tools for tuning your attention: sharpening the image, softening distractions, or shifting perspective. At the same time, letting users pick from different coaching styles shows that there isn’t just one way to get support—whether you prefer quick hints, detailed guidance, or something in between, you stay in charge.
